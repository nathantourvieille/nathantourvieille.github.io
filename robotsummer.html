Project 2: Robot Summer

A practice run before the competition

Over six weeks, I designed and built two autonomous robots capable of playing a real-life version of the game *Overcooked* with my three teammates. The goal was to score points by assembling food items and delivering them to a serving area marked by a 1 kHz IR beacon. Our strategy focused on assembling burgers as quickly as possible.

  • Robot 1 (Ingredient Picker): Navigated to ingredient areas, picked up individual ingredients, and delivered them to the cooking surface for the second robot.
  • Robot 2 (Burger Assembler): Stacked ingredients in a chute to assemble burgers and dispensed them onto a plate at the serving area.

Competition time

On competition day, we consistently assembled two burgers in two minutes. Unfortunately, a malfunction in the semi-finals resulted in a fourth-place finish.

My Contributions

Mechanical Contributions:

  • Constructed the chassis from laser-cut MDF and waterjet aluminum.
  • Designed and 3D-printed the ingredient chute and fabricated claws using Delrin, MDF, cardboard, plastic, and sheet metal.
  • Utilized Onshape for design and manual tools, including a lathe and mill, for fabrication.

Software Contributions:

  • Programmed ESP-32s for control and integrated sensors for line following and station detection.
  • Developed alignment software to handle overshoot and tuned PID parameters for precision.
  • Addressed noise during startup with robust filtering techniques.
  • Implemented ESP-Now for inter-robot communication to coordinate tasks effectively.
